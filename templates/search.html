Empowering Students around the Planet to Solve Biology

All disease can be cured, given the correct tools.

Disco identifies potential treatments to treat incurable diseases, in less than 60 seconds.


Drug Discovery is Expensive

It costs upwards of one billion dollars, and three to twenty years on average, for researchers to identify promising medication candidates.

Disco aims to chagne this, by empowering students with the ability to identify new potential treatments for pressing diseases, for free, within seconds.


Powerful Tech Core

Disco traces how a specific disease affects certain genes, and subsequently associates these genes to a medication target.

Disco combines three different datasets, and when fully uploaded, will incorporate 500+ diseases, 800+ genes, and 19,000+ gene-drug relationships.

Current development is focused on leveraging quantum computing with traditional neural networks, to more rapidly traverse Disco's database and better recognize promising treatment.

Development History


v0.1 Initial Release (January 2023)
  • Implemented graph traversals
  • Proof of Concept with Grover's algorithm (3 qubits) to sort results
v1.0 Cloud Deployment (March 2023)
  • Web app deployed to cloud
  • Users can create accounts to bookmark relevant results
v1.1 Quantum Boost (planned, April 2023)
  • Quantum results complement silicon computer results
  • Implement quantum algorithms to simulate near-simultaneous traverse the dataset
  • Train neural networks to improve graph traversal heuristics
v2.0 Quantum Boost+ (planned, Fall 2023)
  • Quantum-first results
  • Transpose existing dataset on a quantum computer for near-simultaneous traversals

Who is Building Disco?
